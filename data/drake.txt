Sir Francis Drake was an English explorer and privateer best known for making the second circumnavigation of the world in a single expedition between 1577 and 1580 (being the first English expedition to accomplish this). 
He is also known for participating in the early English slaving voyages of his cousin, John Hawkins, and John Lovell. 
Having started as a simple seaman, in 1588 
he was part of the fight against the Spanish Armada as a vice admiral.
